Social Media Optimization (SMO)

ASocial Media Optimization also known as SMO was a word that didn’t exist 30 years ago. The only social media that existed before the internet was TV in which you could spend thousands upon thousands of dollars on a thirty second advertisement. In today’s world a multitude of avenues exist on various social platforms that all require different techniques. Let’s take a deep dive. Your main social media strategy should consider the following (Odden 2012) Objectives- This is utilizing social media for brand awareness. Listening- Listening is when you are alert and aware of what your customers are talking about and your business objectives. Audience - Finding out who your audience is, and how they think. Participation- What is your online presence? Measurement- Keeping track of progress, what social media tactics are working and which SMO tactics are not working. There are many social media outlets, the most popular being Pinterest, Youtube, Facebook,Twitter, and Instagram. Depending on what niche your business is in will be a large determining factor in what social media tactics you should deploy in your business. For instance, if you are in the weight loss niche and are selling a weight loss program Youtube and Pinterest may be your best strategy. If you are selling financial services Pinterest would be a terrible social media outlet to choose as the audience is not inclined to financial service pins. Once you have chosen a social media outlet you will want to seek out the best optimization for your social media optimization. These tactics are rapidly changing, so you must pay attention to the date of the content. A common tactic is Search engine optimization or SEO and it has been found that if your brand does not have a strong social media presence you will not be found in the search engines as often causing less traffic (Jahapatra). This makes your social media technique even more important as SEO is a great source of free traffic.
