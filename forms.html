Social Media Optimization (SMO)

HTML forms are the bread and butter for many websites. Forms are where business will collect data about future clients, gather emails to create a list, or compile a list of data about their prospective customer. It’s important that the forms are easy to use, are not easily spammed, and work all of the time. If the form is not properly formatted and well suited for the web browser this could lead to missed business opportunities, money, and vital data. This article will go into the basics of styling forms with CSS to make them appealing to the user and some common HTML fundamentals. Forms can be made up of texts, email, radio buttons, file button, reset button, and many more options. A form can be designed with the server side language PHP or it can be designed with HTML on the client side.
