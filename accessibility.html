Accessibility

Top 5 Reasons to Implement Accessibility on your Website What exactly is accessibility? Accessibility is when you make your website accessible to people with disabilities. These disabilities can include, but not limited to hearing, sight, cognitive, and movement (Henry, 2019) Below are the top reasons to implement accessibility on your website.  

  • Better SEO Google loves the user experience. They haven't implemented penalty for not including accessibility on your website yet, but in the near future Google may decide to penalize sites that to not cater to those with disabilities. Not only does making your site accessible, it also gives people that don’t have a disability an advantage. Things like faster site speed and better mobile experience can also boost your page rankings. According to MOZ web accessibility overlap on the below items ( Lipay, 2016) Video transcription Image captioning Image alt attributes Title tags Header tags (H1, H2, etc) Link anchor text On-site sitemaps, table of contents, and/or breadcrumbs Content ordering Size and color contrast of text Semantic HTML
  • Future-Proof   We have no idea what the future holds. There may be a device created that humans do not have to physically search content, therefore making your site accessible may also make your site easier for these devices to read. For example, when you upload a picture to your website and properly label the picture, you are telling google search engine what the picture is, and you are also telling future devices, spiders, crawlers what the picture is. This could help your business out in unforeseen ways.  
  • Wider Audience There are over 1 billion people with disabilities that are needing to gather information from the web. When you include people with disabilities you have a wider range of people. You could even make a niche that includes ONLY people with disabilities, but could branch out to the masses due to the great accessibility you have included on your website!
    •  
  • Prevention of Legal Issues There are new laws being passed everyday. It’s best if you make your website accessible to everyone now to save time later in the future. The internet is getting more and more regulated and it only makes sense for the next move to be towards improving experience for people with disabilities.
